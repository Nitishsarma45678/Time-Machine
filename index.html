📷 Photography in Your Birth Year

When Film Was
Still Everything

In 2002, the world was still shooting on film. Digital cameras existed — but they were expensive, clunky, and couldn't match the warmth of a well-developed roll of Kodak 400. Most families documented life with disposable cameras bought from a chemist shop, or prized 35mm SLRs passed down from parents. You'd shoot 24 or 36 frames, drop the roll at a photo studio, wait two days — and finally hold those precious prints in your hands for the first time. No preview. No delete. Just light on silver, and hope.

"Photography is the art of frozen time — the ability to store emotion and feelings within a frame." — Meshack Otieno
